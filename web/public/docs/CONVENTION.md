# bitchan — Ratifying Convention Record

The constitution is ratified by a convention of the 42 Framers (the `framer-council`
skill). Each delegate evaluates the charter in character, against his own documented
constitutional tests, and votes **RATIFY / RATIFY WITH AMENDMENTS / REFUSE**.

---

## First Reading — Quorum (13 of 42)

**Tally:** 0 RATIFY · **13 RATIFY WITH AMENDMENTS** · 0 REFUSE
**Determination:** RATIFIED, conditional on the amendments below. Unanimous support,
unanimous conditions. Notably **even Mason did not refuse** — bitchan put a Bill of
Rights *first* (Article I), the very omission that made him refuse in 1787.

### The council's required amendments (its "Bill of Rights"), by support
1. **Constitute the Senate's *powers* now** (≈all 13) — don't defer the checks; route impeachment + treasury oversight + `do-not-serve` re-affirmation to direct citizen vote until the chamber is constituted at a threshold. → *Art V.2*
2. **Nail down Article VII with real numbers** (≈12) — min account-age, capped cost, an open path, invite-graph slash rules; settled before any election. → *Art VII*
3. **Sever the President from the purse** (≈8) — fee schedule Governable not presidential; ceiling **and** floor as written numbers; fixed stipend, no cut. → *Art III.4, VI*
4. **No blanks or cross-references for immutable bounds** (≈5, Williamson) — write the actual figures. → *Parameters appendix*
5. **Harden `do-not-serve`** (Mason, Sherman) — expires unless renewed by an independent body, President excluded. → *Art III.3*
6. **Protect speech from the everyday mop** (Madison, Carroll, Mason) — log every `hide`; never `hide` opinion or criticism of moderators. → *Art III.2*
7. **An explicit rights declaration in Article I** (Mason) — rights stated as rights, not only mechanisms. → *Art I.6*

### The dissent, in voice
> **Mason:** *"A mechanism is not a right… I will not sign a document that buries the
> right of free press inside a structural mechanism while leaving its Senate deferred
> and its fee ceiling unnamed."* — Held, then persuadable: grant the rights
> declaration, the `do-not-serve` check, and the Senate's powers, and he signs.

**All seven amendments are incorporated into Constitution v2**, along with the
founding-period (Washington model) and year-end-election decisions.

## Final Ratification — Full 42 (on Constitution v2)

**Tally:** 21 RATIFY · 21 RATIFY-WITH-AMENDMENTS · 0 REFUSE.
**Determination: RATIFIED** — unanimous assent, conditional on the second-round
amendment package (incorporated into Constitution **v3**).

Waves 1–3 (architects, compromisers, Southern bloc) ratified outright — confirming
the first-reading amendments had landed. Waves 4–6 were run **hardened** (priming
stripped, explicit license to refuse) and unanimously ratified *with amendments*,
converging on one theme: **the charter granted and bounded powers but provided no
*process* to contest their exercise — it was missing a judiciary.**

**The dissent held but was won:** Mason (persuaded by the rights-first structure),
**Gerry — who refused the actual U.S. Constitution — conditioned rather than
refused**, and Randolph the swing came over (*"I will not foreclose the Union before
it draws breath"*).

### Second-round amendment package (by support → incorporated in v3)
1. **A judiciary / right of contest** (≈10) — any citizen may contest a `hide` /
   `do-not-serve` / `slash` / result before an independent body that can **void** it
   (Blair, Ingersoll, Livingston, McHenry, Carroll, Mason, Randolph, Mifflin). → *Art X*
2. **Close the custodian-checks-president loop** (≈7) — re-affirmation & slash by
   citizen supermajority, not the President's appointees. → *Art III §3, VII §3*
3. **Write every quorum/fraction/denominator in-text** (≈7). → *Art V §1–2, Parameters*
4. **Open the treasury books** (≈3) — immutable on-chain ledger + sortition auditors. → *Art VI §4*
5. **Raise the account-age floor to 60 days** (≈4). → *Parameters*
6. **Immutable-ize conscience/opinion** (Mason, Bassett, Carroll). → *Art I §7*
7. Sharp catches incorporated: rate-limit on citizenship cost (Baldwin); slash needs a
   hearing (Mason); founder `do-not-serve` bounded/suspended (Bassett, Gerry); named
   on-chain transition registry on a clean roll (Mifflin, McHenry); citizen
   early-election trigger at ≥T/2 (Baldwin); Gerry's 60%-no-quorum recall valve;
   "injurious" defined by enumerated grounds (Gerry, Ingersoll).

**Held against amendment** (founder's calls): term stays **immutable 1 year**
(Jenifer's governable-term rejected); fee/cost stays **rate-limited, no absolute
ETH ceiling** (Morris/Few's ceiling declined — staking-trap), softened by extending
the rate-limit to the citizenship cost.

> **Gerry, in voice:** *"I have been taught by experience the danger of the levelling
> spirit — and the equal danger of the man who, finding himself alone at the wheel,
> persuades himself that his own virtue is security enough for a free people; grant
> these amendments, and I shall most cheerfully add my name."*

The constitution is ratified. v3 is the founding charter.
